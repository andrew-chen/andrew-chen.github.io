Relationships First, Then AI: What the Pandemic Taught Me About Teaching in the AI Era

Audience/Group Discussion session · The 3rd Annual MN Thought Leaders Summit (AI4MN) · Apollo High School, St. Cloud, MN · June 15, 2026

This session draws on my teaching experience from 2019 to the present — through the pandemic, a period of AI-skeptical rebuilding, and successive phases of AI integration — to argue that relationships, trust, and community are not extras in learning environments: they are preconditions. The central question is whether AI use supports or substitutes for the work of learning, and how educators can evaluate that distinction using a relationships-first lens.

The session uses the Eight Dimensions of AI Literacies (Gunder et al., 2024) as an organizing framework, mapped against a support-versus-substitution distinction. The accompanying handout is designed to be useful independently of the presentation as a practical reference for evaluating AI use in educational contexts.
